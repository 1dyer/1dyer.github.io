# 第四天
<font size=3>In his book The <font color=orange>Tipping</font> Point, Malcolm Gladwell argues that social <font color=orange>epidemics</font>” are <font color=orange>driven in large part</font> by the actions of a tiny <font color=orange>minority</font> of special <font color=orange>individuals</font>, often called <font color=orange>influentials</font> who are unusually informed, <font color=orange>persuasive</font>, or well connected.</font>

<font size=3>在马尔科姆·格拉德威尔（Malcolm Gladwell）的书《引爆点》中，他提出社交流行现象很大程度上是由一小部分特殊个体的行动驱动的，这些个体通常被称为"有影响力的人"，他们在信息、说服力或人际关系方面异常出色。
</font>

<font size=3 color=blue>

minority n.少数群体、少数派

individual n.个体

influential n.有影响力的人或事物

persuasive adj.有说服力的

</font>

***
<font size=3>For a social epidemic to <font color=orange>occur</font>, however, each person so affected must then influence his or her own <font color=orange>acquaintances</font>, who must in turn influence theirs, and so on; and just how many others pay attention to each of these people has little to do with the <font color=orange>initial influential</font>.</font>

<font size=3>要发生社会流行现象，每个受影响的个体必须影响自己的熟人，而这些熟人必须进而影响他们自己的熟人，依此类推；而每个人受多少其他人的关注与最初的有影响力的人没有多大关系。</font>

influential  adj.有影响力 有影响力的人或事物
</font>
